About Us

Want to improve your fluency in a language but can find someone to practice with? At languageXChange we're dedicated to helping you find someone to have a chat in another language. You meet up online or face to face. You can choose the amount of time you'd like the session to last: 20 mins, 40 mins or 1 hour.

How it works

  • Answer some questions

    We ask questions about things you care about

  • See people who match your preferences.

    Sometimes it's easier to have a conversation in a second language about topics you are interested in.

  • Set a time

    Once you've found someone who you'd like to do a language exchange with, send them a message! Pick the amount of time you'd like the session to last for.

  • Correction feedback

    There is the option of sending feedback on 3 errors made (or more if you really want to).

  • Learning journal

    Here you can select or write your own goals for the month. After each session, we ask you what would you focus on next time to improve your fluency? What can can you specifically do in the meantime to improve in that area? Although it is strongly encouraged, the learning journal is optional.

How it all started

While studying at a language school, I would often walk past a board called Language Exchanges. It was messy brimming with notes, pinned on top of another. I thought, wouldn't be great if this was available to everyone, not just for people at this language school? The idea for langXchange was born.

Tips

Check out our tips to ensure the best experience

Tip 1: You get what you give

Let’s say you’re learning Spanish and are meeting a native Spanish speaker who’s learning English.

Tip 2: Remember your partner is not a teacher

Even if your language exchange partner is a teacher in real life, remember they are to practise a language and NOT for a paid service.

Tip 3: Focus on Communication

One thing to keep in mind during meetings is that the main goal is conversation practice. So it’s important to let your partner speak freely, even if they make mistakes.

Tip 4: Be ready

Use the conversation cards tool. You can both choose topics or auto-generated questions from our conversation cards tool
